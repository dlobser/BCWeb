The Science: Four Pillars of DOPE

Thalamocortical Rhythms ▼

Rhythmic visual stimulation increases coupling between the thalamus and cortex. When light flashes at specific frequencies, it engages thalamic circuits to coordinate widespread cortical activity, helping your brain maintain focus and coherence.

Key Research

Flickering light at ~10 Hz induces stronger connectivity between the lateral geniculate nucleus (visual thalamus) and higher-order visual cortex. This thalamocortical synchronization acts like a drummer keeping the cortical orchestra in time, enabling coordinated brain activity without pharmacological substances.

The human perceptual system processes fractal patterns with exceptional fluency.
