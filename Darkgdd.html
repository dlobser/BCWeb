Dark

A short, weird sci-fi horror game about a child running errands at the end of the world.

Horror Mystery Sci-fi Sonar Short-form

Concept

You are four years old. A portal to another dimension has opened, unleashing death and madness. Daddy is in his room with Mommy, and the door is closed. You can hear Mommy moaning and screaming. Daddy's voice comes through the door — calm, specific, asking for things. You don't know what he's doing in there. You don't understand it. You just know he needs you to fetch things, and you do, because he's Daddy.

Cables run out from under the door into the living room, where they connect to a collection of machines. From the center of the machines, a booming electrical storm tears upward through the ceiling — a tornado of light and energy, visible from outside the house. The machines are always on. You never go in the room. You never see Daddy or Mommy until the very end.

You go outside. Everything is dark. You navigate with an acoustic ping — a sonar device that briefly illuminates the world through sound. Daddy said to keep an eye out for your sisters.

Pillars

  • Sound is sight. The sonar ping is the primary sense. Pinging is dangerous.
  • The lore arrives in pieces. Understanding leaks in at the edges, never stated.
  • Dread over gore. Most scares are false alarms. The real ones land harder for it.
  • Short and strange. Compact, no padding. Every mission means something.

The Three Questions

What is Daddy doing to Mommy?
What happened to your sisters?
What is being built in the living room?

Some of these will be answered in fragments as you go. Some may never be answered directly. The machine behind the door is the spine of the mystery.

Core Loop

  1. Daddy's voice comes through the door. You have a mission.
  2. Go outside into the dark. Navigate with your sonar ping.
  3. Complete the mission objectives — area-based, multi-step.
  4. Return to the house. Daddy may or may not acknowledge you.
  5. Mission progress is saved. You always restart from the house.

Sight, Sound, and the Ping

  • Passive vision: a faint outline of the ground close around you.
  • Noise-emitters in the world give off light proportional to the sound they make.
  • The ping is your active sense — a short pulse that reveals geometry briefly.
  • Pinging has cost: the Scarecrow will get you if you ping it. The Evil Sister can see you when you ping.
  • The flashlight is the only holdable light source — steady light, no ping risk, limited range.

The Looking Rule

If you're not looking at the scary thing, it can't hurt you.

The central survival trick: know where the threat is, and make sure you aren't facing it. This inverts normal horror instincts — the player's impulse is to look, and looking is often what gets you killed.

The Sisters

Daddy tells you to keep an eye out for your sisters. As you explore, the narrative of what happened to them unfolds: some have died in horrible ways, some have gone insane, some may attack you. Some have been turned into trees.

The trees are everywhere — dark silhouettes in the field. They look ordinary until you ping them. Then the sonar flash reveals twisted, suffering shapes. And sometimes you hear it: a voice from inside the bark.

"Help me."

The tree-sisters are lore first and gameplay second. They appear as discoveries, not objectives. Whether the player can do anything to help them is an open question.

Missions

Get the Mail

Walk to the mailbox at the end of the drive. Bring back whatever arrived — schematics, instructions, a component for the machine. The road is dark. Something may be watching.

Get Bloodfruit

Bloodfruit grows from the trees fed by the animals butchered in the barn. Go to the barn, find what's left, follow the runoff to the trees. Pick the fruit. Don't look at what's on the barn floor too long.

Collect Electricity

The forest of power lines crackles with loose current. Bring a collector and gather enough charge. The lines hum and spark. Things may be drawn to the light.

Start the Generator

The generator is out back. Find it, find what it needs, get it running. The storm overhead does not help with navigation.

Go to the Church

Walk to the church at the edge of the property. Get the Bible from inside. The church is not empty.

Fix the Basement Electrical

The lights have gone out. Go to the basement and fix whatever broke. It is very dark in the basement. Ping carefully.

Monsters

The Scarecrow

Appears in different positions on the map each time. It looks like a silhouette. If you ping it — directly, with your sonar — it gets you. No warning. No second chance. Don't ping silhouettes that look too still.

Tumbleweeds

They look like tumbleweeds. They are not tumbleweeds. They roll through the dark and if one hits you, you go down and wake up somewhere else on the map. Disorienting by design.

Evil Sister

She stalks you. She can only see you when you ping — your sonar pulse lights you up for her. As long as you stay dark, she stays blind. She will get close. She will breathe. As long as you can't see her, she can't see you.

Giant Monster

It walks around in the distance. It is very large. It seems like it might hurt you. It never does. It is there to remind you that the world outside is not on your side.

Trees (Sister-Trees)

Appear as silhouettes in the dark. If you ping them, the sonar flash reveals twisted, reaching forms — your sisters, transformed. They do not attack. They ask for help. Whether they can be helped is unclear.

Holes

Holes in the ground. You can fall into them. Navigate carefully in new areas.

Jump Scares

Fear of jump scares is the goal — real scares should be rare. One at the very beginning so the player knows what to be afraid of. After that, mostly false alarms: building tension without payoff, again and again, until the real ones land.

The Scarecrow Tutorial (real). Early on, you ping a still silhouette. It was the Scarecrow. It gets you. Now you know.
The Rustling Door (false). Something pushes against a closed door from inside. You ping. Nothing is there.
The Sister Tree (unsettling, not lethal). You ping a tree and the sonar flash shows a twisted face. A voice: "Help me." It does not attack.
The Tumbleweed (startling). A tumbleweed hits you from nowhere. You wake up somewhere else. Startling, not deadly.
The Giant Nearby (false). You ping and the Giant is much closer than expected. It turns and walks away. Nothing happens.
The Generator Wakes (scripted). The generator starts and everything shudders — lights flash, a roar, the storm overhead flares. Camera briefly taken over. Then quiet.

Tableaus

Scattered environmental set-pieces: the aftermath of horrible things. Carcasses in the barn split open and drained. Trails of blood leading out to the trees. The charred silhouette of something large near the power lines. A sister's shoe in the field, alone.

Tableaus are wordless lore. They imply sequence, causality, and horror without ever naming what happened. The player builds the picture from fragments.

Scoring

  • Time. How long it took to complete each objective.
  • Secrets found. Environmental discoveries, sister-trees heard, hidden lore.

Score is shown at the end. No in-mission feedback — just how it felt, then the tally afterward.

Game Systems

Mission System

Missions use area markers, not point markers — a region on the map lights up, not a waypoint arrow. Missions may have multiple objectives that unlock sequentially or in parallel. Daddy's voice or a note in the mailbox starts each mission. Progress is tracked as mission state, not world state.

Interaction

Click to interact with objects or pick them up. Items go directly to inventory. The flashlight is the only holdable item — equipping it means holding it, which means it occupies your hand and stays on until you put it away. Other items (fruit, components) are just carried invisibly.

Event System

A global broadcast event bus fires on mission state changes, area entries, item pickups, and monster contacts. Scripted moments — the generator waking, finding a sister-tree, reaching the church — can take over the camera briefly for a fixed sequence before returning control. Events can trigger sounds, music shifts, Daddy's voice, or map notes.

Monster Behaviors

  • Scarecrow: Repositions between pings. Ping it directly = instant death. Detected by raycasting from its position to ping origin.
  • Tumbleweeds: Patrol loose paths. On contact, trigger a "tumbled" state that relocates the player.
  • Evil Sister: Follows the player at a distance. Enters "sight" state only when the player pings — she can then close in. Returns to blind-follow on no ping.
  • Giant Monster: Wanders a fixed route in the far background. No aggression. Scales with distance to stay always-looming.

HUD / Compass / Map

A minimal compass sits in one corner. A small map view (togglable) shows explored area with mission area markers. An inventory panel shows held items. A notes panel accumulates mission logs and found lore — this is the primary narrative vehicle. No waypoints. No enemy indicators.

Save System

You always start at the house. Mission progress is saved between sessions — objectives completed stay completed. There are no mid-mission saves and no checkpoints outside the house. If you die, you wake up at the house with mission progress intact but position reset.
